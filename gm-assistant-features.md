
## Combat & Encounters

1. **Initiative tracker** — drag-to-reorder turn order, with HP, AC, and active conditions visible per combatant.
2. **Stat block manager** — searchable library of monsters/NPCs you can drop straight into an encounter.
3. **Condition & duration tracker** — auto-reminds when an effect ends ("poisoned: 2 rounds left," "save at end of turn").
4. **Legendary/lair action reminders** — pings you at the right initiative counts so you never forget them.
5. **Encounter builder** — assemble a fight and get a live difficulty/CR readout against the party.
6. **Hidden HP & "bloodied" flags** — track real HP secretly while showing players only vague status.

## NPCs & World

7. **NPC roster** — portraits, voice/accent notes, personality quirks, and current location.
8. **Random NPC generator** — instant name, trait, motivation, and mannerism for when players talk to "that random guard."
9. **Faction & relationship web** — visual graph of who hates/owes/serves whom.
10. **Lore wiki** — cross-linked pages for locations, items, history, with backlinks.
11. **In-world calendar & timeline** — track dates, moon phases, scheduled events, and what's happened so far.

## Players & Table Management

12. **Player dashboard** — at-a-glance passive Perception, AC, saves, languages, and resistances for the whole party (GM reference only).
13. **Reveal window** — a dedicated, screen-shareable window where the GM controls exactly what's currently visible: drop in an image, letter, map crop, or text, and toggle it on/off. The GM's other windows stay private; only this one is shared.
14. **Recording & playback module** — capture session audio, or import pre-recorded voice lines / ambient loops, and play clips on cue, routed through the shareable window. Good for prepared NPC voice lines, narration, or "found tape" moments.
15. **Spotlight tracker** — gentle nudge showing who hasn't had a meaningful moment recently (GM reference only).
16. **Player-facing display mode** — a clean version of the broadcast window showing only what players are allowed to see.

## Atmosphere & Audio/Visual

17. **Ambient music player** — scene-tagged playlists (tavern, dungeon, boss fight) with crossfade.
18. **Sound effect soundboard** — one-tap door creaks, thunder, sword clangs, screams.
19. **Mood/lighting presets** — shift the player display's color/visuals to match the scene tone.

## Dice & Rules

20. **Dice roller with macros** — saved rolls, advantage/disadvantage, and a "roll hidden" GM mode.
21. **Searchable rules reference** — SRD/system rules with instant lookup.
22. **Rulings log** — record house rules and past on-the-fly decisions so you stay consistent.
23. **Custom random tables** — roll on your own loot, weather, rumor, or encounter tables.

## Prep & Notes

24. **Session notes** — timestamped, taggable, searchable later.
25. **Scene/beat planner** — sketch the intended flow of the session as movable cards.
26. **Quest & plot-thread tracker** — open threads, clues planted, and which are resolved.
27. **Recap generator** — pull last session's notes into a "previously on…" summary.
28. **Improv prompt button** — generates a complication, twist, or NPC when players go off-rail.

## Maps

29. **Battle map with fog of war** — reveal areas as players explore.
30. **Token management** — drag tokens, snap to grid, attach HP/conditions to them.
31. **Ping/pointer tool** — highlight a spot for players ("the trap is *here*").

## Pacing & Time

32. **Session timer & pacing bar** — real-time clock with optional act/scene budgets.
33. **Break reminder** — nudge to pause every X minutes.

## Investigation & System-Specific (esp. Call of Cthulhu)

34. **Sanity tracker** — per-investigator SAN, with quick SAN-loss rolls.
35. **Clue/investigation board** — pin clues and draw connections (the "red string" corkboard).
36. **Skill-check helper** — quick opposed/threshold rolls tuned to the system in play.

## Cross-Cutting Utility

37. **Command palette / global search** — one hotkey to find any NPC, rule, note, or sound instantly.
38. **Loot & treasure generator** — system-appropriate rewards on demand.
39. **Layout presets** — saved window arrangements per game system (D&D combat layout vs. CoC investigation layout).
40. **Campaign archive** — searchable history across all sessions, so "what was that innkeeper's name in session 3?" is one query.

---

## Design Note: The "One Broadcast Window" Principle

Because players have no accounts and only see what the GM screen-shares, there is exactly **one shareable broadcast window**. These modules take turns being the thing shown in it:

- **#13 Reveal window** (images, letters, text)
- **#16 Player-facing display mode**
- **#29–31 Battle map, tokens, ping/pointer**
- **#14 Recording & playback** (audio routed through the share)

Everything else (#1–12, #15, #17–28, #32–40) stays GM-eyes-only by default.
