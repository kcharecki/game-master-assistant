Mara Whitlock

Ally Harbourmaster's Clerk · Currently at Wharf Records Office

Mara Whitlock has kept the Blackwater harbour ledgers for eleven years, and she remembers every name that has ever crossed a manifest. Thin, sharp-eyed, and perpetually ink-stained to the second knuckle, she is the sort of person the town forgets exists — right up until it needs to know who was on which boat, when.

Appearance & Voice

Late forties, silver-streaked hair pinned in a severe knot, wire spectacles that catch the lamplight and hide her eyes. She wears the same charcoal cardigan regardless of season, sleeves pushed to the elbow. Her voice is dry, clipped, and faintly amused — she speaks the way she files paperwork: precisely, and only when necessary.

Manner & Quirk

Answers questions with questions. Taps a pencil twice against the desk before delivering anything she considers important. Keeps a private ledger of "things people don't want written down" — separate from the official one — and will trade from it, carefully, for favors of comparable weight.

Motivation

Mara wants the harbour to make sense. She has watched three ships log false cargo weights in the last year alone and has quietly built a case she's too frightened to bring to Sgt. Halloway directly. She will help investigators who can be discreet — and drop them cold the moment they aren't.

Secrets (GM Only)

The falsified manifests all trace back to a single vessel — the Grey Dawn — chartered quietly by Randall Voss. Mara doesn't know about the Society yet, only that "someone with money" is moving unlabeled crates through the north slip on moonless nights.

She is Deacon Marsh's second cousin, estranged since childhood, and has never once mentioned the Marsh name aloud. If pressed with genuine kindness (not interrogation), this can be pried loose — she is desperate to tell someone.

Sanity note: if shown the north-slip manifests alongside the Marsh family tree, Mara makes the connection herself mid-conversation. Keeper may call for a 0/1 SAN reaction roll on her behalf — played for the players to witness, not for them to lose.
